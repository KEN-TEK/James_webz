About the James Webb Space Telescope

In the vast expanse of space, beyond the reaches of our solar system, lies a universe of untold wonders. The James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) is humanity's most ambitious and powerful space observatory, designed to unveil the secrets of the cosmos.

On December 25, 2021, the James Webb Telescope was launched, aiming to explore our origins and the existence of life elsewhere.

JWST Missions

The James Webb Space Telescope was launched to explore the origins of life on Earth and to search for signs of life beyond our planet. It studies the atmospheres of planets and moons in our solar system and beyond, investigating the evolution of galaxies throughout their life cycles.

Discoveries of the James Webb Telescope

Did You Know?

  • The universe is about 13.8 billion years old.
  • A day on Venus is longer than a year on Venus.
  • There are more stars in the universe than grains of sand on all the Earth's beaches.
  • One million Earths could fit inside the sun.
  • The nearest galaxy, Andromeda, is about 2.537 million light-years away from Earth.
